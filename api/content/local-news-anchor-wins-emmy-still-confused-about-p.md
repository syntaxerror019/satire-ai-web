Efrem Graham’s journey from NBC Page to Emmy-winning CBN News anchor is a masterclass in corporate ladder-climbing, where “Page” apparently means “person who accidentally invents the ‘breaking news’ format during a coffee spill.” Born in Jersey City but raised in the shadow of NYC’s corporate communications behemoth, Efrem’s career began by fetching coffee for executives who thought “behind-the-scenes” meant “behind the coffee machine.” His rapid rise to ABC’s Toledo station was fueled by a single, unshakable belief: if you’re not getting Emmys for your morning news, you’re not doing it right. Now, after accidentally rebranding NBC as “The Corporation That Communicates,” Efrem’s next move? Applying for a job at a reality TV show about corporate communications. Because nothing says “journalism” like a man who once thought “Page” was a type of sandwich.
