By Debbie Fetter and Andrea Glenn, two nutrition professors who clearly have better things to do than research how much protein humans need, we've got some advice on where to get it from. And honestly, it's all about variety - you know, because one type of protein is just not enough for the human body, apparently.

Protein, in case you didn't know, is made up of amino acids, and there are 20 different kinds that your body uses to build things like muscles, hair, nails, and even your own tiny human self. But don't worry if you haven't been eating enough of it - just head on over to the nearest protein bar vendor or buy a tub of whey powder online!

Beef is a great source of protein, but also comes with a side of saturated fat and cholesterol, because who needs a healthy heart anyway? Just ask the Cleveland Clinic, they'll tell you that smaller portions are probably best - but what's "smaller" even mean when it comes to beef?

Pork, on the other hand, is high in protein and B-complex vitamins, but only if you're not eating processed meats like bacon or ham. Those things are basically just meat-flavored cardboard with added sodium.

Eggs are a good source of protein, but be careful because they might raise your cholesterol levels - unless you're one of those special snowflakes who can eat seven eggs per week without it affecting your heart health in the least.

Fish is another great option, especially if you can afford to eat it regularly. But don't worry if you can't, just stick with canned fish and pretend you're still getting the nutrients from fresh salmon.

And then there's beans - those magical little things that are basically just tiny protein factories, but also come with a side of digestive issues because fiber is hard on your insides.

Tofu is another complete protein source, but only if you don't have any soy allergies. And please, for the love of all things holy, do not eat it in place of actual food - that's just lazy.

Protein powders are basically just a convenient way to get more protein into your diet, unless you're one of those people who can afford to buy a whole bunch of different brands and flavors. In that case, go ahead and spend all your money on supplements!

In conclusion, it's clear that humans need variety in their protein sources - but also not too much, because excess protein is just bad for you, apparently. And honestly, the best thing you can do is just eat a balanced diet with lots of different foods and don't worry about it too much. But hey, if you want to overcomplicate things with all sorts of supplements and powders, be our guest - we're sure your healthcare costs will skyrocket in no time!
